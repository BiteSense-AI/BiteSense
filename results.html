Immediate Actions


Remove the tick

If the tick is still attached, use fine-tipped tweezers to carefully pull the tick out without twisting or crushing it. Clean the bite area with soap and water.

Monitor for symptoms

Be vigilant for signs of infection or disease in the coming days.

When to Seek Medical Advice

If you experience any unusual symptoms such as fever, joint pain, fatigue, or a rash at the bite site, contact a healthcare provider promptly. Early treatment can prevent complications from tick-borne illnesses.

Treatment Guidance

For mild irritation, over-the-counter creams like hydrocortisone may help with redness or swelling. Antihistamines can relieve itching if necessary.

Stay Informed

To better understand the risks in your area, consult the interactive map below for tick species distribution and disease prevalence near you. Prevent future bites by using insect repellent and wearing long sleeves when in tick-prone environments.

Your results show you got most likely got bit by a Tick!


Discovering a tick bite can be alarming, especially with the risk of tick-borne diseases like Lyme disease, ehrlichiosis, and Rocky Mountain spotted fever. It’s important to act quickly and understand the type of tick that bit you. To help you identify the tick and assess potential risks, we’ve provided an interactive map displaying different tick species and the regions where they are commonly found.

Ticks are small, but they can transmit serious diseases. Depending on the type of tick, the symptoms of a bite may vary. Some common signs to watch for include:

  • Redness or a rash around the bite area
  • Fever, chills, or muscle aches
  • Fatigue or joint pain
  • Swelling or tenderness near the bite

If you notice any of these symptoms after a tick bite, it’s important to consult a healthcare provider as soon as possible.

How to Use the Interactive Map

To further explore tick activity in your area, and to get a sense of the types of ticks that may have bitten you, take a look at the interactive map we’ve provided below. This tool will help you identify the specific type of tick, its geographical range, and the diseases it might carry.

For a better view, be sure to click the “full screen” icon in the bottom right corner of the map, so you can explore the information in more detail.
